IITBNF EQUIPMENT CATALOG
=========================
This document describes the major equipment categories and instruments
available at the IIT Bombay Nanofabrication Facility (IITBNF). It is used
by the AI Assistant to answer detailed questions about facility tools,
their capabilities, and typical use cases.

DEPOSITION SYSTEMS
------------------
PECVD (Plasma Enhanced Chemical Vapor Deposition): Used to deposit thin
films such as silicon nitride, silicon dioxide, and amorphous silicon at
relatively low substrate temperatures using RF plasma to enhance the
chemical reaction. Common applications include passivation layers, hard
masks, and dielectric layers in MEMS and semiconductor devices.

LPCVD (Low Pressure Chemical Vapor Deposition): Deposits high-quality,
uniform thin films (polysilicon, silicon nitride, silicon dioxide) at
low pressure and higher temperatures than PECVD, producing denser films
with better step coverage. Commonly used for gate oxides and structural
layers in MEMS.

Sputtering Systems: Physical vapor deposition technique that deposits
metal and dielectric thin films (aluminum, gold, titanium, platinum,
tungsten, oxides) using ion bombardment of a target material. Used for
metal contacts, interconnects, and seed layers.

Thermal/E-beam Evaporation: Deposits metal films by heating source
material in vacuum until it evaporates and condenses on the substrate.
Used for lift-off metallization, ohmic contacts, and optical coatings.

LITHOGRAPHY SYSTEMS
--------------------
Spin Coaters: Apply uniform thin layers of photoresist or other polymer
solutions onto substrates by spinning at controlled speeds. A foundational
step before optical or e-beam lithography.

Mask Aligners: Used for photolithography — UV exposure of photoresist
through a photomask aligned with existing wafer patterns. Supports contact,
proximity, and projection exposure modes depending on resolution needs.

E-beam Lithography: Direct-write lithography system using a focused
electron beam to pattern resist without a physical mask, enabling
nanometer-scale feature definition for research-grade and prototype
devices.

ETCHING SYSTEMS
----------------
RIE (Reactive Ion Etching): Combines chemical reactivity of plasma species
with physical ion bombardment to anisotropically etch silicon, oxides,
nitrides, and polymers with high directionality.

ICP Etching (Inductively Coupled Plasma): High-density plasma etching
system capable of deep, high-aspect-ratio etches (e.g., deep silicon
etching, Bosch process) used for MEMS structures and through-silicon vias.

Wet Etching Bench: Manual and semi-automated wet chemical etching stations
for isotropic etching of oxides, metals, and silicon using standard wet
chemistries (BHF, KOH, TMAH, metal etchants).

CHARACTERIZATION SYSTEMS
--------------------------
SEM (Scanning Electron Microscope): Provides high-resolution surface
imaging down to nanometer scale using a focused electron beam, used for
inspecting feature dimensions, surface morphology, and fracture cross
sections.

TEM (Transmission Electron Microscope): Provides atomic-to-nanometer
scale imaging of thin samples by transmitting electrons through the
specimen, used for crystal structure and defect analysis.

AFM (Atomic Force Microscope): Maps surface topography at sub-nanometer
vertical resolution using a cantilever probe, used for roughness
measurement and nanoscale surface characterization without requiring
a vacuum environment.

XRD (X-Ray Diffraction): Determines crystal structure, phase composition,
and strain in thin films and substrates by analyzing diffraction patterns
of X-rays.

XPS (X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy): Surface-sensitive technique used
to determine elemental composition and chemical bonding states within
the top few nanometers of a sample.

EDX (Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy): Often paired with SEM, used
for elemental composition analysis of a sample via characteristic X-rays
emitted under electron bombardment.

THERMAL PROCESSING SYSTEMS
-----------------------------
Diffusion Furnaces: Used for high-temperature processes such as thermal
oxidation, dopant diffusion, and annealing of silicon wafers in controlled
atmospheres.

RTA (Rapid Thermal Annealing): Provides fast, controlled heating and
cooling cycles for short-duration high-temperature processes such as
dopant activation and silicide formation, minimizing dopant diffusion
compared to furnace annealing.

METROLOGY SYSTEMS
-------------------
Profilometers: Measure surface topography and step height profiles using
either contact (stylus) or non-contact (optical) methods, commonly used
to verify etch depth or film thickness.

Ellipsometers: Measure thin film thickness and optical constants (refractive
index, extinction coefficient) by analyzing the change in polarization of
reflected light.

IV/CV Measurement Systems: Used to characterize electrical properties of
devices and test structures — current-voltage and capacitance-voltage
behavior of diodes, transistors, and capacitor structures.

ACCESS AND BOOKING NOTES
---------------------------
Each piece of equipment has a designated System Owner responsible for its
operational status and maintenance coordination. Access to most equipment
requires an approved Tool Permission, granted by the relevant System Owner
or Faculty Incharge after training. Equipment usage must be booked through
the slotbooking portal, and a session report should be submitted after
each use describing observations, parameters used, and any issues
encountered.
